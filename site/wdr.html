WDR Paper Computer

The WDR Paper Computer is a simple, educational computer architecture designed to teach the fundamentals of computer science and assembly language programming. It was developed by the Westdeutscher Rundfunk (WDR) broadcasting company in Germany. The WDR Paper Computer uses a minimalistic instruction set and operates on a paper-based model, allowing users to simulate the behavior of a computer without the need for actual hardware by using just paper and pen.
